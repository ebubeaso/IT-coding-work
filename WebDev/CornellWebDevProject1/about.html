EBUBE'S DANCE PERSPECTIVE: Growing As A Dancer

About Me

Born and raised from Providence, Rhode Island, I started learning about dance during my junior year back in high school. I wanted to do something special for prom, so I decided to learn how to dance (I was the only member in my family who could not dance at the time). From there, I taught myself some basic grooves and footwork to learning different types of hip hop dance styles including:

  • Popping
  • Waving
  • Tutting
  • Breaking/B-boy
  • Rocking/Footwork

I have also incorporated other styles including dancehall, salsa, house, and tricking to create my unique style as a well-rounded dancer. Every day I look for ways to improve; I continue on improving my body control, learn new movements, and even make variations of the different moves I have learned. However, dance isn't only about moves. I practice listening to music and move along with it, ensuring that I capture the feelings emanating from the song.

Through dance, the space around me is my canvas, my body is the paintbrush and the music is the paint. With these components I am capable of creating my unique form of visual art to showcase to the world! Whether I inspire one person or a multitude of people, it will be an honor to have my dance perspective mean a lot to another dancer working on their craft.
